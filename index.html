Well differentiated papillary mesothelial tumor: a new name and new problems

高分化型乳頭状中皮腫瘍：新しい名称と新たな問題

Andrew Churg・Francoise Galateau-Salle

Abstract / 要旨

Well-differentiated papillary mesothelial tumor (WDPMT, formerly called well-differentiated papillary mesothelioma) is a morphologically distinctive lesion composed of expansile papillae with a myxoid core covered by a single layer of generally bland mesothelial cells. Whether some WDPMT are precursors of invasive mesothelioma is uncertain, and this question is confounded by shallow biopsies of ordinary diffuse mesotheliomas that have superficial areas resembling WDPMT as well as by misinterpretation of some cases of mesothelioma in situ.

Genetic analyses on a very small number of published cases of peritoneal WDPMT have shown a variety of mutations/copy number losses that do not overlap at all with those that are found recurrently in invasive mesotheliomas. The newly described entity of mesothelioma in situ usually appears as a single layer of mesothelial cells that have lost BAP1 by immunostaining, but sometimes is papillary and produces a morphologic mimic of WDPMT.

We propose that, at least in the peritoneal cavity where most WDPMT occur, there are two morphologically identical but functionally distinct lesions: one is true WDPMT, a process that is probably benign, and the other is papillary mesothelioma in situ with the configuration of WDPMT. For that reason immunostaining for BAP1, and if necessary MTAP or CDKN2A FISH, should always be performed on cases with the appearance of WDPMT. It is possible, but speculative, that the small number of reports in the literature which describe invasive mesothelioma arising from WDMPT are actually describing invasive mesothelioma arising from mesothelioma in situ that looks like WDPMT.

高分化型乳頭状中皮腫瘍（WDPMT、以前は高分化型乳頭状中皮腫と呼ばれていた）は、 形態学的に特徴的な病変であり、 粘液様のコアを持つ拡張性乳頭状構造が、 一般的に単調な中皮細胞の単層で覆われている。 一部のWDPMTが浸潤性中皮腫の前駆病変であるかどうかは不確かであり、 この問題は、通常の広範性中皮腫の浅い生検や、 WDPMTに類似した表層領域を持つもの や、一部の中皮腫上皮内病変（in situ）の誤った解釈により複雑化している。

腹膜WDPMTの発表された極めて少数の症例における遺伝子解析では、 様々な変異/コピー数損失が示されており、 これらは、 浸潤性中皮腫で繰り返し発見されるものとまったく重複しない。 最近記述された中皮腫上皮内病変（in situ）は 通常、中皮細胞の単層として現れ、 免疫染色によってBAP1を喪失しているが、 時に乳頭状となりWDPMTの形態学的模倣を生じることがある。

Introduction / はじめに

Well-differentiated papillary mesothelial tumor (WDPMT), formerly known as well-differentiated papillary mesothelioma, is a histologically distinctive mesothelial tumor of uncertain malignant potential found in the pleura, peritoneum, and tunica vaginalis. Both the latest Thoracic Tumors and Female Genital Tumors WHO books recommend using WDPMT rather than well-differentiated papillary mesothelioma as a diagnostic term because surgeons and oncologists frequently view the "mesothelioma" part of "well-differentiated papillary mesothelioma" as an indication that these are overtly malignant neoplasms that should be treated in the same fashion as ordinary invasive diffuse mesotheliomas (MM) (for example, ref. 3).

However, changing the nomenclature only partially addresses the clinical problem, because morphologic overlaps with invasive mesotheliomas, the significance of an invasive component in WDPMT, genetic separation from invasive mesotheliomas, the relationship of WDPMT to the newly described entity of mesothelioma in situ, and, most important, how WDPMT behave remain unresolved questions. This article will briefly highlight these issues and recommend some approaches for pathologists.

高分化型乳頭状中皮腫瘍（WDPMT）は、 以前は高分化型乳頭状中皮腫として知られていた 組織学的に特徴的な中皮腫瘍であり、 悪性度が不確かな 胸膜、腹膜、精巣鞘膜に見られる腫瘍である。 最新の胸部腫瘍および女性生殖器腫瘍に関するWHO書籍では、 WDPMTを使用することを推奨している 高分化型乳頭状中皮腫よりも 診断用語として、 外科医や腫瘍専門医が頻繁に見なすため 「高分化型乳頭状中皮腫」の「中皮腫」の部分を 明らかな悪性腫瘍の指標として 同様に治療すべき 通常の浸潤性びまん性中皮腫（MM）と （例えば、参考文献3）。

しかし、命名法の変更だけでは 臨床的問題に部分的にしか対処できない。 なぜなら、浸潤性中皮腫との形態学的重複、 WDPMTにおける浸潤性成分の意義、 浸潤性中皮腫からの遺伝的分離、 WDPMTと最近記述された中皮腫上皮内病変との関係、 そして最も重要なこととして、WDPMTがどのように振る舞うか という未解決の問題が残っているからである。 本論文ではこれらの問題を簡潔に強調し、 病理医のためのいくつかのアプローチを推奨する。

Clinical and gross pathologic findings / 臨床的および肉眼的病理所見

The largest individual WDPMT series comes from Sun et al. who published 75 cases of their own and found a total of 180 cases in the literature as of 2019; of these 135 were peritoneal, 37 pleural, 6 in the tunica vaginalis, and 2 in hernia sacs. In the same year Vogin et al. reported another 56 peritoneal cases (without pathologic details), although 9 of these were described as having "fatty infiltration" raising a suspicion that they represent misdiagnosed ordinary invasive MM. In the summary prepared by Sun et al., peritoneal cases in women outnumbered those in men by 6:1, whereas pleural cases showed a roughly equal female:male distribution.

Clinically, pleural cases generally are symptomatic and are associated with a pleural effusion; at thoracoscopy there typically are multiple small nodules over the affected pleura. In contrast, in the peritoneal cavity WDPMT is usually found incidentally during surgery for another process. Only a very small fraction of patients present with abdominal pain or ascites and are found to have WDPMT as the cause. Such patients sometimes have bowel wall or mesenteric thickening on imaging. In a minority of cases peritoneal WDPMT are associated with multicystic mesotheliomas, adenomatoid tumors, and endometriosis. On direct examination, peritoneal cases may have single nodules, a few nodules, or occasionally innumerable nodules.

最大規模のWDPMT症例シリーズは Sunらによるもので、 彼らは自身の75例を発表し、 2019年時点で文献中に合計180例を見出した。 そのうち135例が腹膜、 37例が胸膜、6例が精巣鞘膜、 2例がヘルニア嚢に発生したものだった。 同年、Voginらは報告した 別の56例の腹膜症例を（病理学的詳細なし）、 そのうち9例は「脂肪浸潤」があると記述され、 誤診された通常の浸潤性MMである疑いが生じた。 Sunらによって準備された要約では、 女性の腹膜症例は男性の6倍であったのに対し、 胸膜症例ではほぼ同じ女性：男性比率を示した。

臨床的に、胸膜症例は一般的に症状があり、 胸水を伴う。 胸腔鏡検査では、通常、多数の小結節がある 罹患した胸膜に。 対照的に、腹膜腔では WDPMTは通常、偶然発見される 別のプロセスのための手術中に。 患者のごくわずかな割合だけが 腹痛や腹水を呈し、 その原因としてWDPMTが見つかる。 このような患者は、画像検査で腸壁や腸間膜の肥厚を示すことがある。 少数例では、 腹膜WDPMTは関連している 多嚢胞性中皮腫、腺腫様腫瘍、子宮内膜症と。 直接検査では、 腹膜症例は単一の結節、 いくつかの結節、または時には無数の結節を有することがある。

Microscopic findings / 顕微鏡的所見

WDPMT is typically composed of papillae with expansile myxoid cores covered by a single layer of generally bland, flattened to cuboidal mesothelial cells (Fig. 1). Sometimes the expansile papillary stroma of WDPMT is fibrotic/hyalinized, presumably a finding in old lesions. WDPMT stain with traditional mesothelial markers such as calretinin, WT-1, cytokeratin 5/6, and D2-40, and should be negative with traditional carcinoma markers. An important pitfall to note is that PAX-8, a marker frequently positive in ovarian and other gynecologic tumors, is frequently positive in WDPMT, typically in peritoneal but occasionally in pleural lesions. PAX-8 positivity was observed in 94% of WDPMT in the series of Sun et al. and 61% of WDPMT in the series of Xing et al.). These numbers suggest that PAX-8 positivity in WDPMT is more frequent than in MM, although in our experience PAX-8 staining of both WDPMT and peritoneal mesotheliomas is dependent on antibody clone and antibody type (polyclonal stains more frequently than monoclonal).

Another marker that has sometimes been proposed as positive in WDPMT but negative in mesotheliomas is L1CAM (CD171). However, Inaguma et al. reported positive L1CAM staining in 70% of mesotheliomas and Itami et al. in 53% of mesotheliomas, so L1CAM probably has little diagnostic value in this setting.

While the appearance of WDPMT is characteristic, there are nonetheless three important microscopic confounders. One is a superficial sample of an ordinary invasive diffuse mesothelioma; MM sometimes have papillary areas near free surfaces and such areas can closely mimic WDPMT (Fig. 2). Cytologic detail can be helpful: prominent nucleoli and, particularly, cytologic pleomorphism should raise a suspicion of invasive mesothelioma rather than WDPMT, although prominent nucleoli can, rarely, be seen in tumors that are (apparently) WDPMT. The operator's description or CT scan reports may be informative if there is unequivocal evidence of malignancy.

A second confounder is ovarian type serous tumors, including ovarian serous surface papillomas, serous borderline tumors, and, occasionally, low grade serous carcinomas, the vast majority in the peritoneum cavity but rarely intratesticular/paratesticular. All of these lesions can have a papillary structure that mimics WDPMT, but typically have columnar cells with considerable cytologic atypia in the borderline and low grade serous carcinomas. Most important, they stain strongly with broad spectrum carcinoma markers such as MOC-31, claudin-4, and BerEP4. They are usually calretinin negative but WT-1 and PAX-8 positive.

The third, and crucial, confounder is mesothelioma in situ, which can take on the microscopic appearance of WDPMT (see section 5 below)

Significance of invasive foci in WDPMT / WDPMTにおける浸潤巣の意義

In most WDPMT the proliferating mesothelial cells are confined to the surface of the papillae, but occasional tumors demonstrate invasion of mesothelial cells into the fibrovascular stalks that support the papillae or sometimes below the level of the stalks (Fig. 3). These lesions are different from the type of ordinary mesothelioma in which there is surface formation of structures that mimic WDPMT (such as Fig. 2), because WDPMT with invasive foci have the overall structure of WDPMT, not diffuse mesothelioma.

Churg et al. described 20 WDPMT with invasion confined to the stalks which they labeled "well differentiated papillary mesothelioma with invasive foci." In that series tumors with invasive foci were associated with a distinct tendency toward multifocality and recurrence, the latter seen in eight patients. No patient developed metastatic disease, and only one patient was reported to have died of disease, but no histologic confirmation of the type of tumor present at death was available.

Trpkov et al. reported a case of tunica vaginalis WDPMT that by illustration had invasive foci, and the patient was well without recurrence 6 years later. Butnor et al. found superficial invasion in two pleural cases; one did not have followup and one had persisting disease at 1 year. In the peritoneal series reported by Sun et al., six cases had invasion of mesothelial cells deep to the stalk but none of these patients developed disseminated mesothelioma. Malpica et al. noted that 1 of 26 peritoneal WDPMT showed shallow invasion into the underlying (ovarian) tissue but that this was not an adverse prognostic finding.

Reported cases in which clearly documented and unequivocal diffuse invasive mesotheliomas arose in a WDPMT are few. Torii et al. reported a case of multifocal pleural WDPMT that was treated with extrapleural pneumonectomy; deep invasive disease was seen in the resection specimen. Of interest, this patient had a pleural effusion and the illustrated cytologic appearance of the effusion was that of a mesothelioma, suggesting that the WDPMT was really mesothelioma in situ because mesothelioma in situ has been reported to produce a picture identical to invasive mesothelioma in effusion samples.

Shimizu et al. described a patient with an incidentally discovered WDPMT lesion in a lung cancer resection specimen; there was invasion of mesothelial cells from the WDPMT into the underlying stroma and the invasive process morphologically looked like a mesothelioma. Costanzo et al. illustrated a case that morphologically started as pleural WDPMT and ended as metastatic mesothelioma 13 years later. Burrig et al. described a similarly well illustrated case in which mesothelioma developed 5 years after diagnosis of a peritoneal WDPMT. Galateau-Salle et al. reported two patients with pleural WDPMT and no evidence of invasion at biopsy, in whom invasive lethal mesothelioma was diagnosed after 10 years. Sun et al. show a nicely illustrated case in which invasive mesothelioma developed 15 years after the diagnosis of WDPMT. Washini et al. described a case in which invasive mesothelioma developed 7 years after the diagnosis of WDPMT; however, while the invasive tumor looks like a mesothelioma, from their illustrations the original tumor is suspicious for mesothelioma in situ rather than WDPMT.

Genetics / 遺伝学

There is very little in the literature on genomic analyses of WDPMT, and all of this is confined to peritoneal tumors. Stevers et al. reported ten cases and found only mutually exclusive CDC42 (two cases) or TRAF7 (eight cases) missense mutations. Their TRAF7 mutations all localized at the C-terminus of the protein. Of interest, to get adequate sequencing depth Stevers et al. combined multiple separate WDPMT tumors in two patients but still found only a single CDC42 or TRAF7 mutation in each patient, which suggests that multifocal WDPMT represents clonal spread. Nine cases did not have copy number losses or gains.

In contrast, Shrestha et al. analyzed 5 cases and found recurrent missence mutations of EHD1, ATM, FBXO10, SH2D2A, CDH5, MAGED1, and TP73, all present in either 4/5 or 5/5 cases. No case had a mutation of either TRAF7 or CDC42, although one showed copy number loss of TRAF7. The WDPMT was strongly enriched for C>A transversion substitutions, a pattern that is not seen in MM. There were a variety of copy number losses and gains but no consistent pattern across the 5 tumors.

None of the WDPMT in either the Stevers or Shrestha paper had abnormalities in genes found to be recurrently mutated or deleted in MM such as BAP1, SETD2, NF2, LATS1/2, CDKN2A/B), although it should be noted that TRAF7 mutations have been described in a few peritoneal mesotheliomas. Nemoto et al. reported a lesion claimed to be WDPMT in which loss of heterozygosity in NF2 was present; however, their illustrations do not look at all like WDPMT and probably represent an ordinary MM.

WDPMT as a manifestation of mesothelioma in situ / 中皮腫上皮内病変の症状としてのWDPMT

Mesothelioma in situ (MIS) was recently defined as single layer of flattened to cuboidal mesothelial cells that had lost BAP1, and sometimes MTAP, by immunochemical staining. A single case of MIS with CDKN2A deletion but BAP1 retention has also been described. However, the original description of ten cases of MIS included one (pleural) case with both flat mesothelium and a process morphologically very similar to WDPMT. We have subsequently seen a number of cases of peritoneal MIS that have both flat single-layered mesothelium and areas identical to WDPMT (Fig. 4).

Other authors have described "WDPMT" with BAP1 loss. Lee et al. stained eight peritoneal cases that microscopically looked like WDPMT. BAP1 was lost in three and these three had synchronous (but spatially separated) or metachronous invasive mesothelioma; genetic analysis showed an identical pattern between the WDPMT and the invasive tumor in each case. We believe that these cases really are examples of MIS that morphologically look like WDPMT.

Dacic et al. performed whole-exome sequencing on two cases of MIS which immunohistochemically showed BAP1 loss; in both cases, the analyzed areas were partially flat and partially papillary, with the latter areas having the appearance of WDPMT. The pleural case had copy number loss and LOH in the 3p21 region that harbors BAP1. The peritoneal tumor showed a somatic BAP1 splice site mutation and BAP1 copy number loss. No other somatic mutations, indels, or somatic copy number abnormalities were identified; in particular, none of the recurrent mutations or recurrent copy number losses seen in ordinary MM were present. Dacic et al. suggested that BAP1 mutations/deletions may be the earliest abnormality in invasive mesothelioma. Since BAP1 loss/mutation is always a marker of malignancy in mesothelial proliferations these data strongly imply that some WDPMT, or at least papillary processes that look like WDPMT, are precursors of invasive mesothelioma, an idea that contradicts the (very limited) published genomic analyses of WDPMT described above.

It appears likely that MIS always becomes invasive, although the time from biopsy diagnosis of MIS to invasive disease is unclear and can be quite long. In the series reported by Churg et al., invasive disease developed in 7/10 patients with an 8th patient showing diffuse pleural thickening on imaging thought suspicious for an invasive mesothelioma at 57 months. For the seven cases with eventual overt MM, the median time to invasion was 60 months with the longest time 92 months, but one patient still had not developed invasive disease at 120 months. In the report of Klebe et al., invasive tumor developed after 4 years 10 months. Fels Elliott described a patient with invasive disease diagnosed at 10 months after biopsy, and Minami et al. a patient in whom no invasive disease could be detected at 9 months. The metachronous case described by Lee et al. had a 10 yr interval between the diagnosis of WDPMT (really MIS) and the appearance of invasive mesothelioma. In the multi-author position paper by Klebe et al., 40% of pathologists reported seeing cases with a greater than 4 year interval between the diagnosis of MIS and the appearance of invasive mesothelioma, although it is not entirely clear whether all authors of that paper were using the same definition of MIS. It should be noted that the papers reviewed in this paragraph largely describe flat and not papillary lesions; in fact, Klebe et al. position paper suggests that WDPMT morphology is not a feature of MIS.

Conclusions: the nature of WDPMT / 結論：WDPMTの性質

The data just described raise a contradictory question: is WDPMT a generally innocuous finding, at least in the peritoneal cavity where most such tumors occur, or it is a form of mesothelioma in situ and a precursor of invasive mesothelioma? The WHO Female Genital Tumors book proposes that WDPMT in the peritoneum are benign and that aggressive behavior is indicative of a missed diagnosis of MM, whereas the Thoracic Tumors WHO book concludes that most pleural WDPMT behave as slowly growing recurrent lesions. A major confounder is that, in many instances, tumors that are called recurrent or (eventually) clinically malignant are not biopsied so whether they are MM is usually not documented.

Detailed followup on the largest series suggests that, in the peritoneal cavity, the vast majority of tumors that look like WDPMT do appear to be functionally benign, even if multifocal. In the pleural cavity the prognosis is more guarded, although as detailed above, it is hard to find many published examples of invasive mesothelioma developing from WDPMT lesions in either site.

Genomic analyses, albeit based on few and only peritoneal cases, imply that WDPMT are distinct neoplasms that have no relationship to ordinary diffuse mesotheliomas. However, this idea is contradicted by the observation of BAP1 loss by immunochemistry and BAP1 mutations/deletions in cases of mesothelioma in situ that are morphologically identical to WDPMT and by the occasional case in which tumors that morphologically appear to be MM have been documented to arise in WDPMT, as described above.

We propose that, in fact, there are two different lesions that can look microscopically like WDPMT: true (probably benign, at least in the peritoneal cavity) WDPMT, and WDPMT-like mesothelioma in situ, but it's not possible to separate these entities on routine morphology. The relative frequency of these two different lesions is unclear and the number of reported cases is too small to draw definite conclusions. As noted, Lee et al. found 3/8 cases with WDPMT morphology that had BAP1 loss by immunochemistry, whereas 3 other reports totaling 18 cases did not find BAP1 loss, although in one series the comment was made that BAP1 retention was often patchy in WDPMT; this phenomenon can occasionally be seen in mesothelioma in situ (Churg A, Galateau-Salle, unpublished data) and probably represents spread of a malignant clone.

What is not known at this point is what fraction of WDPMT actually represent MIS, nor is it known what fraction of MIS look like WDPMT. The authors of this article have seen around 30 examples of the latter, but because these are consultation cases, there is extreme selection bias and one can't use these numbers to estimate the real frequency of MIS that looks like WDPMT. However, it is possible that such lesions are more common than anyone realizes because the frequently long lead time for MIS to develop into invasive mesothelioma exceeds the followup time for some fraction of reported patients. For example, in the report of Malpica et al., there was only 1 recurrence (incidentally discovered) in a series of 25 peritoneal WDPMT, but the median followup time was 32 months. In the series of Sun et al. the median followup time for 46 patients was 29 months and the one apparent transformation of WDPMT into invasive mesothelioma took 15 years. In the 22 patients described by Daya and McCaughey, one patient died at 7 years with what appears to be diffuse peritoneal tumor by description. A number of other individual cases with very long times between the diagnosis of WDPMT and invasive mesothelioma have been listed above in "Significance of invasive foci in WDPMT". It is conceivable that, in fact, all of the cases reported as WDPMT that eventually "turned into" or recurred as invasive mesothelioma are really examples of mesothelioma in situ mimicking WDPMT. At the moment this idea is speculative but is potentially testable using archival material.

ただ今記述したデータは矛盾する質問を提起する： WDPMTは、一般的に無害な所見なのか、 少なくともそのような腫瘍のほとんどが発生する 腹膜腔では、 それとも中皮腫上皮内病変の一形態であり 浸潤性中皮腫の前駆体なのか？ WHO女性生殖器腫瘍の書籍は、 腹膜のWDPMTは良性であり、 攻撃的な行動は MMの見逃された診断を示すと提案している。 一方、WHO胸部腫瘍の書籍は結論付けている ほとんどの胸膜WDPMTは 緩慢に成長する再発性病変として振る舞うと。 大きな交絡因子は、多くの場合、 再発または（最終的に）臨床的に悪性と呼ばれる腫瘍は 生検されないため、それらがMMであるかどうかは通常記録されていないことである。

我々は、実際、2つの異なる病変があると提案する 顕微鏡的にWDPMTのように見える： 真の（少なくとも腹膜腔では恐らく良性の）WDPMT、 とWDPMT様の中皮腫上皮内病変、 しかし、通常の形態学ではこれらの実体を区別することはできない。 これら2つの異なる病変の相対的頻度は不明であり 報告された症例数は 確定的な結論を導くには少なすぎる。 前述のように、Leeらは免疫化学によってBAP1喪失を持つ WDPMT形態の3/8症例を発見した、 一方、他の3つの報告では合計18例で BAP1喪失は見られなかった。 しかし、1つのシリーズでは、コメントがなされた WDPMTではBAP1保持がしばしば斑状であるという。 この現象は時に中皮腫上皮内病変でも見られることがあり （Churg A、Galateau-Salle、未発表データ） おそらく悪性クローンの拡散を表している。

Conclusions: recommendations for diagnosis / 結論：診断のための推奨事項

Given this situation, we suggest that at a minimum, a BAP1 immunostain should be performed on any lesion that looks like WDPMT; if BAP1 is retained, then MTAP immunohistochemistry or CDKN2A FISH should be the next step. It should also be remembered that the combination of BAP1/MTAP/CDKN2A investigation will not detect all invasive mesotheliomas and the same conclusion presumably applies to mesothelioma in situ, particularly since the sensitivity of CDKN2A FISH (and by implication MTAP immunochemistry) is known to be fairly low in the peritoneal cavity. A potentially helpful finding is that all the MIS cases we have seen that look like WDPMT also have flat mesothelioma in situ which usually looks like a single layer of bland cuboidal mesothelial cells (see Fig. 4C); however, benign reactions can also look like a single layer of bland cuboidal mesothelial cells, so appropriate immunohistochemical or FISH detection is still required.

This approach may appear to be overkill, but even if MIS constitutes only a few percent of lesions that look like WDPMT, MIS probably requires aggressive therapy before it becomes invasive. On the other hand, truly benign WDPMT probably requires no therapy at all beyond just local excision, and in fact, radical therapy for such tumors is often associated with adverse outcomes. What we suggest, when dealing with a lesion that looks like WDPMT and has retained BAP1 and retained MTAP or no CDKN2A deletion, is that the case be signed out as WDPMT. However, a comment should be added that in all likelihood the lesion is benign and requires no further therapy, but followup is warranted because there is a small risk that such lesions still could represent MIS.

The significance of stalk invasion that originates in a WDPMT remains unclear and is not necessarily an adverse finding, but in such cases BAP1/MTAP/CDKN2A FISH analysis is crucial. Even if this analysis is negative, a statement that patients with WDPMT with invasion should be carefully followed would probably be appropriate in a surgical pathology report.

この状況を考えると、我々は少なくとも、 BAP1免疫染色を実施すべきだと WDPMTのように見える病変に対して提案する。 BAP1が保持されている場合、次のステップは MTAP免疫組織化学またはCDKN2A FISHであるべきである。 また、覚えておくべきことは BAP1/MTAP/CDKN2A調査の組み合わせが すべての浸潤性中皮腫を検出するわけではないことも 同じ結論が中皮腫上皮内病変にも当てはまると推測される、 特に腹膜腔におけるCDKN2A FISHの感度が （および暗示的にMTAP免疫化学） かなり低いことが知られているため。 潜在的に役立つ所見は、 我々が見てきたWDPMTのように見えるすべてのMIS症例は、 平坦な中皮腫上皮内病変も持っているということで、 これは通常、単層のように見える 単調な立方体状中皮細胞の（図4C参照）。 しかし、良性反応も見える場合がある 単調な立方体状中皮細胞の単層のように、 そのため、適切な免疫組織化学的またはFISH検出はまだ必要である。

このアプローチは過剰に見えるかもしれないが、 MISがわずか数パーセントを構成するだけだとしても WDPMTのように見える病変の、 MISは浸潤性になる前に 積極的な治療を必要とする可能性がある。 一方、真に良性のWDPMTは おそらく治療をまったく必要としない 局所切除以外の。 実際、そのような腫瘍に対する根治的治療は しばしば有害な結果と関連している。 我々が提案するのは、病変を扱う場合、 WDPMTのように見え、BAP1が保持され、 MTAPが保持されているか、CDKN2A欠失がない、 症例をWDPMTとして報告することである。 しかし、おそらく病変は良性であり、 さらなる治療は必要ないが、 追跡調査が必要であるというコメントを追加すべきである そのような病変がまだ MISを表す可能性が小さいため。

WDPMTから発生する 茎浸潤の意義は 依然として不明であり、必ずしも不利な所見ではないが、 そのような症例ではBAP1/MTAP/CDKN2A FISH分析が極めて重要である。 この分析が陰性であっても、 浸潤を伴うWDPMTを持つ患者は 慎重に追跡すべきであるという声明は、 外科病理報告書において適切であろう。
